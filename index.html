Flame-Weeding Tractor Implement

Novel mechanical design for weed control on small-scale farms · Werkr Tractors Co-op · 2025

3D CADLaser CuttingMachiningArduinoDesign for Manufacturing

CAD Model

Underside View

Attached to Tractor

In Action

This is a project I designed entirely from scratch during my co-op at Werkr Tractors, a large flame-weeding implement that kills young weeds before planted seeds germinate. It was my most complete end-to-end engineering challenge: CAD design, manufacturing, pipeline engineering, and field testing, all in one.

The central design constraint was minimizing welding while maximizing bent sheet metal, reducing cost and simplifying fabrication. The implement uses a forklift propane tank feeding 6 individual burners, each with a spark plug and coil controlled by an Arduino. Stainless steel heat shields contain the flame, increasing efficiency and allowing faster travel speeds.

As a first prototype, I standardized the heat shield geometry with extra bolt holes, keeping all shields identical and eliminating laser-cutting error. Seeing it run in the field for the first time was highly rewarding.

Electric Tractor Implement Crane

Prototyping and Design for Manufacturing · Werkr Tractors Co-op · 2026

3D CADLaser CuttingMIG WeldingFEA

CAD Design

FEA Stress Analysis

Finished Product

Zinc-Plated & Installed

After observing how difficult implement swaps were, I designed a front crane allowing a single operator to lift, swing, and hook up implements far more quickly. This was inspired by the Tilmor tractor design.

Built from bent laser-cut sheet metal and MIG welded, this design was considerably cheaper than metal tubing. A large center cutout reduces weight. A rope-and-crank system handles loads up to 250 lbs.

An FEA study in SolidWorks validated structural integrity before fabrication. The finished crane was zinc-plated for corrosion and rust protection.

Electric Tractor Rollbar

Safety structure, roof mount, and antenna housing · Werkr Tractors Co-op · 2026

3D CADLaser Cutting3D Printing

Rollbar CAD

In Context on Tractor

Protects the driver, supports the roof, and mounts antennas at the tractor's highest point. Originally designed in metal tubing, I redesigned it with laser-cut bent sheet metal to allow flat-pack shipping, not possible with the welded original.

Three main structural tubes were retained; everything else is laser cut and subsequently nut-and-bolt assembled. A 3D-printed antenna and WiFi router housing at the rear uses balls and springs to rotate and clear low overhangs, a passive safety mechanism.

Full Camp Map Overview

Wash House & Dining Hall

Night View with LEDs

A half-year passion project helping campers and retreat groups understand the layout of Silver Lake Mennonite Camp. Every structure was individually modelled to scale in Onshape and printed on a Bambu Lab X1C. Everything from large buildings, to small ball bins and kayaks was included.

The model consists of nearly 500 printed parts, epoxied lake surfaces, and CNC-routed paths (Scienci LongMill). Buildings lit at night in real life have individual LEDs mounted inside, switchable as a group. Mini campfires light up giving the model an added ambiance.

Managing a complex multi-part assembly, coordinating dozens of unique print settings, and integrating wiring throughout, all while maintaining visual accuracy and structural integrity.

Periodic Table of Elements

120 uniquely designed element boxes · Art piece and learning tool · Personal Project

Onshape CADBlenderFDM PrintingWoodworkingArduino

Full Periodic Table

Elements by Groups

Elements by Density

A 4-foot interactive periodic table designed as both an art piece and a learning tool for high school classes, aimed at creating excitement around chemistry and visualizing elemental patterns.

Each of the 120 element boxes was individually 3D modelled with a unique symbol designed from scratch. Each box includes a diffusing layer halfway through to evenly spread light from the LEDs behind. The title and LED holes were CNC routed into the plywood backing.

Shown at several schools, the table allows students to visualize properties like melting point, electronegativity, and density through individually controllable, colour-coded LEDs which are controlled by a 3D-printed box housing an Arduino.

Electric Tractor Printed Circuit Board

First PCB design, success on first revision · Werkr Tractors Co-op · 2026

PCB DesignCircuit DesignWiring

Finished PCB — Werkr Tractor Computer Box V1

PCB Layout in EDA

Installed in Control Box

As the autonomous tractor's control box grew more complex, debugging became harder and connections less reliable. I designed a custom PCB to reorganize the wiring, moving away from DIN rail connections and focusing on reliability and debuggability.

This was my first PCB design, mentored by professional electrical engineers. I am proud to say it was a success on the first revision, dramatically simplifying the wiring layout and improving system reliability.

Future-proofed with connections for future controls such as remote e-stops and emergency brakes, the PCB positions the tractor for expanded autonomy features.

The electrical backbone of the Periodic Table Wall Art, 120 individually addressable LEDs behind 3D printed element boxes with diffusing layers. All controlled by an Arduino.

Display modes: elements coloured by group, density, melting point, half-life, electronegativity, molecular composition, and various others. This allows students to discover patterns within the table. Scrolling text animations (e.g. "BOIL" for boiling point) add engagement.

Shown at several high schools to generate excitement about chemistry, making an abstract subject tangible and visually compelling.

Periodic Table Wall Art — Woodworking

4 ft structural frame combining woodworking, CNC routing, and finishing · Personal Project

WoodworkingCNC RoutingJoineryFinishing

Finished Wall Art

CNC Routed Board

The 4-foot frame and backing were built entirely from wood. Title lettering and LED holes were CNC routed into plywood with precise positioning to align with each of the 120 3D printed element boxes.

The frame required careful joinery to stay flat and square while supporting the weight of all the printed parts. Surface finishing and painting completed the professional presentation of the piece.

Woodworking acts as the structural and aesthetic foundation for multi-disciplinary builds, providing a stable, beautiful base for electronics and 3D printing layers above it.

Waterfront Epoxy Lake

The camp map base is a large wooden platform with terrain features CNC routed using a Scienci LongMill, paths carved into the surface, lake areas cut out and filled with coloured epoxy for a realistic water effect.

The board frame was hand-built to provide structural rigidity supporting nearly 500 printed parts. Finishing involved sanding, priming, and painting terrain before buildings were installed.
